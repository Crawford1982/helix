Things to Do Near The Kelpies

The best way to make the trip feel bigger, better, and more worth the drive.

Last reviewed 3 April 2026

Home › Things to Do
Planning angle

This page is less about listing everything in Falkirk and more about helping you choose combinations that make sense for real visitors.

The Kelpies are often at their best as part of a better day, not as the entire day. If you have travelled more than locally, pairing them with the wider Helix or another nearby attraction usually gives much better value from the journey.

Best Add-On: The Wider Helix

If you want the easiest improvement to a Kelpies visit, do not rush straight back to the car. The wider Helix gives you more room, better pacing, and a much more family-friendly outing than treating the sculptures as a single quick stop.

Best Nearby Pairing

The strongest classic combination

The Kelpies plus the Falkirk Wheel is still the most obvious and usually the best nearby pairing for first-time visitors. It turns a short landmark visit into a proper Falkirk day.

Wider Helix

Best if you want the simplest no-fuss extension to the visit with more walking space and a better family rhythm.

Falkirk Wheel

Best if you want a more substantial second attraction and a recognisable Falkirk double-header.

Callendar House area

Best if you want more heritage and a softer, slower pace than a pure attractions circuit.

Town stop for food

Best if you mainly want to turn the outing into a relaxed half-day rather than stack up attractions.

Best Itinerary Types

Trip styleBest combinationWhy it works
Young family visitKelpies plus wider HelixSimple, flexible, less rushed
First-time Falkirk dayKelpies plus Falkirk WheelTwo strong headline attractions
Photo-led visitKelpies plus dusk returnAtmosphere matters more than quantity
Relaxed couple outingKelpies plus meal stopLess running around, better pace

Best Advice If You Only Have Half a Day

Choose depth over quantity. One solid pairing or one slower visit is better than trying to cram too many stops into a short window and making each one feel thinner than it should.
