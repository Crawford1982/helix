Hotels, B&Bs, and accommodation in Falkirk and Central Scotland.

Accommodation Near The Kelpies

Falkirk isn't a huge tourist town, but there are decent accommodation options within easy reach of the Helix. Most visitors staying overnight are combining The Kelpies with the Falkirk Wheel and possibly Stirling or a wider Scotland trip.

Hotels in Falkirk

Premier Lodge Falkirk

Budget-friendly and close to the Helix. Walking distance to The Kelpies, which makes it a convenient base. Basic but comfortable — ideal if you just need somewhere clean to sleep.

Distance to Kelpies: Within walking distance

Grange Manor Hotel

A country house hotel near the canal with its own restaurant (Cook's Bar & Kitchen). A step up from budget options, with a bit more character. Good for couples or anyone wanting a more relaxed stay.

Distance to Kelpies: Short drive

Premier Inn Falkirk (Larbert)

Reliable chain option in nearby Larbert. Consistent quality, usually good value if you book ahead. Near the M876/M9 motorway junction, so convenient for getting around.

Distance to Kelpies: ~10 minutes by car

Travelodge Falkirk

Another budget chain option. No frills, but clean and affordable. Handy if you're on a tight budget and just need a bed for the night.

Distance to Kelpies: ~10 minutes by car

B&Bs & Guest Houses

For something more personal than a chain hotel, there are B&Bs and guest houses across the Falkirk area. These tend to offer more character and a Scottish breakfast that'll keep you going all day. Check Booking.com and Airbnb for current availability — options change seasonally.

Booking During Events

Accommodation in Falkirk fills up fast during Kelpies Live (June) and other major events. If you're visiting during event season, book as early as possible. Stirling (20 mins away) is a good alternative if Falkirk is fully booked.

Staying in Stirling

Stirling is about 20 minutes from the Helix by car and has a wider range of hotels and guest houses. It's a great base if you want to combine The Kelpies with Stirling Castle, the Wallace Monument, and the surrounding area. Stirling also has better evening dining options than Falkirk.

Self-Catering & Airbnb

There are self-catering options and Airbnb properties across the Falkirk area, from apartments in town to cottages in the surrounding countryside. This can be more cost-effective for families or groups staying more than one night.

Camping & Campervans

If you're touring Scotland in a campervan, you can park overnight at the Helix Car Park (free, open 24/7) without booking. Facilities are limited — there are no hook-ups or dedicated campervan facilities at the Helix. For a proper campsite, look for options in the wider Falkirk and Stirling area.
