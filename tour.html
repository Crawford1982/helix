What Is the Kelpies Tour?

The Kelpies tour is a 25-minute guided experience that takes you beneath and inside one of the 30-metre horse-head sculptures. A knowledgeable guide walks you through the engineering, construction, and the history behind the sculptures while you stand inside the steel framework that holds them together.

It's the only way to get inside The Kelpies — and it's worth it. Seeing the internal structure up close gives you a real appreciation for how these things were built, and the guides are good at making it accessible for everyone, including kids.

Family Deal

Two children go free with every paying adult. So a family of two adults and two children would pay £17 total rather than £24. A good deal if you're visiting with kids.

Tour Times

Tours run daily from the visitor centre. Standard tour times are:

  • 11:00am
  • 11:30am
  • 1:30pm
  • 2:30pm

Additional tours may be added during busy periods (school holidays, summer weekends). Times can vary, so check with the visitor centre or book online in advance.

Booking Tip

Book in advance during summer and school holidays — tours do sell out. Outside peak times, you can often get tickets on the day from the visitor centre.

What to Expect on the Tour

The tour starts at the visitor centre, where your guide will give some background before walking you to The Kelpies. You'll enter through a door at the base of one of the horse heads and go inside the structure.

Inside, you'll see the steel framework that supports the sculptures — it's surprisingly open and spacious. The guide explains how the thousands of stainless steel plates were fabricated and fitted, the engineering challenges, and the story of the Clydesdale horses (Duke and Baron) that the sculptures are modelled on.

The whole thing takes 25 minutes from start to finish. It's walking-paced and conversational — there's nothing physically demanding about it, though there are some steps involved.

Is the Tour Worth It?

In a word: yes. The Kelpies are impressive from the outside, but going inside gives you a completely different perspective. You get a sense of the scale and engineering that you simply can't appreciate from the plaza. At £8.50 per adult (and with the children's deal), it's good value for what you get.

That said, if you're short on time or visiting on a budget, seeing The Kelpies from outside is still a great experience — and it's completely free.

Private and Group Tours

If you're visiting as part of a larger group, private tours from Edinburgh or Glasgow are available through several operators. These typically combine The Kelpies with other attractions like the Falkirk Wheel and Stirling Castle:

  • Kelpies & Falkirk Wheel private tour: From around £250 for up to 4 guests (approximately 5 hours)
  • Kelpies, Loch Lomond & Stirling Castle: From around £405 for 4 passengers
  • Half-day tours from Glasgow: Various operators offer small group tours

These can be booked through platforms like Viator and GetYourGuide, or through local operators like Private Tours Edinburgh.

Accessibility on the Tour

The inside tour involves some steps, which means it may not be suitable for all visitors. If you have mobility concerns, it's worth contacting the visitor centre in advance to discuss your options — they may be able to accommodate specific needs.

The area around The Kelpies (the plaza and pathways) is fully accessible on flat, surfaced paths.

Visiting The Kelpies at Night?

The sculptures light up from dusk until dawn — free to visit and arguably even more impressive than during the day.
