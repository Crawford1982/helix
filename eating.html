Where to Eat Near The Kelpies

Cafes at the Helix, canalside dining, and restaurants across Falkirk.

Home › Where to Eat

At the Helix

Kelpies Visitor Centre Cafe

The most convenient option — right next to The Kelpies. The cafe serves hot and cold food, including vegetarian and vegan options, with indoor and outdoor seating. Expect sandwiches, soups, baked potatoes, cakes, and hot drinks. The gift shop next door sells treats from local producers too.

Open daily during visitor centre hours (9:30am–5pm April–Oct, 9:30am–4pm Nov–Mar).

The Plaza Cafe

Located at the other end of Helix Park near the adventure playground and lagoon. A good option if you're spending time in the wider park with kids. Serves light meals, snacks, and drinks.

The Horsebox Cafe

A quirky food truck near the park serving ice cream from Falkirk-based Milk Barn, along with drinks and homemade snacks. A nice stop on a sunny day.

Picnic Option

The Helix has plenty of green space and benches. Bringing your own food is a perfectly good option, especially on a nice day. There are bins dotted around the park.

Near the Falkirk Wheel

If you're combining The Kelpies with a trip to the Falkirk Wheel (about 10 minutes' drive), there are food options there too:

  • Falkirk Wheel Visitor Centre Cafe — Indoor seating with views of the Wheel in action. Coffee, sandwiches, soups, and homebaking.
  • Wood-fired pizza oven — An outdoor food option at the Wheel serving freshly made pizza.
  • Airstream food truck — Seasonal street food near the Wheel.
  • Artisan ice cream — Available at the visitor centre.

Canalside Dining in Falkirk

The Forth and Clyde Canal runs through Falkirk and connects The Kelpies to the Falkirk Wheel. Several restaurants and pubs sit along or near the canal:

Cook's Bar & Kitchen

At the Grange Manor Hotel. Canalside location with a varied menu. Good for a sit-down meal after visiting the Helix.

The Copper Top

A popular local pub near the canal with a good selection of food and drinks. Relaxed atmosphere.

Boardwalk

Located by the Falkirk Wheel. Casual dining with canal views. Good for families.

Arnotdale House

A cafe in Dollar Park, a short drive from the Helix. Set in a beautiful park — good for a quieter meal.

Falkirk Town Centre

Falkirk town centre is about 10 minutes from the Helix and has a wider range of dining options:

  • Masala Ram — Well-regarded Indian restaurant near the canal
  • Independent cafes — Several coffee shops and brunch spots in the town centre
  • High street chains — The usual options along the high street and at the retail parks
  • Pubs — A good selection of traditional Scottish pubs serving food

Callendar House Tearoom

If you're visiting Callendar House (about 10 minutes from the Helix), the tearoom there is worth a stop. Set inside the historic house, it serves light lunches, cakes, and afternoon tea in a lovely setting. Good value and popular with locals.

Dietary Requirements

Most cafes and restaurants in the Falkirk area cater for vegetarian diets. The Kelpies Visitor Centre Cafe explicitly offers vegetarian and vegan options. For specific dietary needs (coeliac, allergies), it's worth calling ahead to restaurants outside the Helix.
